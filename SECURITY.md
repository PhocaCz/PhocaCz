# Phoca Security Policy & FOSS Declaration

🌍 **Purely Open Source & Volunteer-Led**
Everything you find here is developed, distributed, and maintained strictly as Free and Open Source Software (FOSS). This software is provided entirely outside the course of any commercial activity as an independent public resource for the global open-source ecosystem.

*   **Strictly Non-Commercial & Public Good:** All extensions are distributed 100% free of charge. No commercial entities control, fund, or operate this group of volunteers. There are no commercial monetization models, paid subscriptions, premium paywalls, or commercial locks associated with this codebase.
*   **Independent Volunteer Contributions:** Development, maintenance, code reviews, and testing are driven entirely by independent, voluntary contributions from this group and the global community.
*   **No Commercial Technical Support:** Technical support is not provided as a paid or commercial service by the volunteers. Community assistance is available solely on a voluntary, non-binding basis via public community forums.
*   **Copyleft Protection (No Proprietary Lock-in):** The projects are proudly licensed under the GNU General Public License (GPL). Anyone has absolute freedom to run, study, share, modify, and redistribute the code, ensuring the software remains free and open for everyone, forever.
*   **Decentralized Infrastructure:** This codebase is hosted in a public, transparent repository to foster open collaborative development and public peer review, ensuring safety and quality through community vigilance.

## Security Stance & Reporting

As a purely volunteer-driven FOSS project, we have no legal obligation under commercial regulations to provide vulnerability disclosure policies. All our code, development, and collaboration reside strictly here on GitHub.

However, **security is highly important to us**. We voluntarily maintain this security policy because we care about the safety of the open-source ecosystem. 

If you discover a security vulnerability in any of our projects, we kindly ask you to report it to us directly so we can voluntarily address it.

**Contact for reporting vulnerabilities:** mailto:phoca.security@gmail.com
